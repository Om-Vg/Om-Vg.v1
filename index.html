About

The Ghost in the Wires and The Code Book by Simon Singh were the two main books that got me hooked with cipher and loopholes on networks. This introduced me to phreaking, network penetration, backdoors in code, and mainly the dark web. The Dark Web's power is intimidating, and I was in awe of the things I witnessed.

As an undergraduate majored in computer science, I have gained a robust understanding of programming, networks, and cryptography. I have tried to obtain a wide variety of experiences throughout my undergraduate career so my graduate studies can build upon this solid foundation. I have worked in the industry and on my project. I have also gained valuable leadership and communication skills by participating in many extra-curricular activities. My diverse background and strong academic performance prepare me well for graduate school. Unfortunately, my UG program did not provide specializations. However, self-learning and keeping myself up-to-date with the current trends helped widen my knowledge in security. Attending courses from Edx, Udemy, and Linkedin Learning introduced me to various techniques that help identify vulnerabilities in a network, address the issues to prevent incidents or attacks, and Learn how to perform penetration testing.
The following courses helped me learn and gain an understanding of the basics :

  • Edx: The University of Washington's: Introduction to Cybersecurity
  • Linkedin's course on Ethical Hacking
  • Udemy's: Learn Ethical Hacking From Scratch

Enrolling in TryHackMe, an online platform that teaches cybersecurity through short, gamified real-world labs, helped me learn the core skills like Web application security, Network security, Basic Linux, and Scripting.
